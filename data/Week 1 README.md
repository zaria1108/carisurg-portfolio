# Week 1 – Project Selection and Literature Review

## Objective

To identify a healthcare problem suitable for AI implementation and conduct an initial literature review to understand existing emergency department triage solutions.

## Activities Completed

- Selected project topic: **AI-Assisted Emergency Department Triage Support System for Resource-Constrained Healthcare Settings**
- Investigated challenges affecting emergency department triage including overcrowding, delayed prioritization, and inconsistent clinical assessment
- Reviewed five academic studies focused on artificial intelligence applications in emergency department triage
- Explored machine learning, natural language processing, ensemble learning, and large language model approaches for supporting triage decisions
- Identified common limitations across existing studies including retrospective datasets, limited explainability, reduced external validation, and concerns regarding real-world clinical implementation

## Literature Reviewed

1. AI-driven triage in emergency departments: A review of benefits, challenges, and future directions
2. Use of Artificial Intelligence in Triage in Hospital Emergency Departments: A Scoping Review
3. TriageIntelli: AI-Assisted Multimodal Triage System for Health Centers
4. Using Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing in Triage for Prediction of Clinical Disposition in the Emergency Department
5. Triage Performance Across Large Language Models, ChatGPT, and Untrained Doctors in Emergency Medicine

## Deliverables

- [x] Confirmed research topic
- [x] Completed preliminary literature review
- [x] Developed initial problem statement

## Reflection

This week focused on understanding how artificial intelligence is currently being applied to emergency department triage. The literature demonstrated strong predictive potential but revealed ongoing concerns surrounding explainability, reliability, and implementation in resource-constrained healthcare environments.
